FEA Analysis of a Steel-Shaft-and-Pulley design

A steel pulley and shaft design was analyzed given some boudary conditions and basic guidance on how FEA software. Given two boundary conditions, a 100 N-m torque was applied to one face of the shaft and the pulley was restrained on one of its cutouts, I had to validate whether the design of the machined parts were subject to failure. Ultimately, through a convergence test, a sensitivty test, and careful analysis of the design, I was able to evaluate the design. This project gave me a solid foundation on the reliability of FEA results along with an understanding on how FEA software works.

Mobile Whiteboard

The mobile whiteboard took plae when a professor asked us to help with the problems that existed within the board. The problems consisted of vibrational sounds when moving the board, uneven wheels, and an unasthetic looking board. Vibrational sounds occured due to the boards slamming on the pipes within the frame when it was in motion. To resolve this issue, felt was placed in between the boards and pipes to get rid of noise all together. For the uneven wheels, set screws, locknuts, and locktite were used in order to properly leevl the wheels. The whiteboard was spray painted and shelves were laser cut and screwed on for supply storage along with being able present an fresh astethic look to the board.

Skeleton liquor Decanter

Given dimensions from a client based drawing, I was tasked to model a skeleton liquor decanter to the best of my ability. This proved to be a challanging task where organic curves made up a majority of the model. Ultimately, with this design project I was able to enahnce my modeling skills by learning advanced surface modeling techniques. Techniques included deleting faces, trimming surfaces, lofting surfaces, thickneing knitted surfaces only after making sure no surfaces are intersecting, etc. In addition, I learned the restraints that come with surface modeling, such as the extent at which the radius of a sketch can curve.
